Driving under the influence (DUI) is a serious offense with far-reaching consequences, especially for active-duty military personnel. For service members, a DUI can jeopardize careers, security clearances, and even personal freedom. GEICO, as one of the largest auto insurers in the U.S., has specific policies and support systems in place to assist military personnel facing DUI charges. This article explores how GEICO handles DUIs for active-duty personnel, the legal and financial implications, and how service members can navigate this challenging situation.

The Impact of a DUI on Military Personnel

A DUI conviction carries severe penalties for civilians, but for active-duty service members, the stakes are even higher. The military enforces its own disciplinary measures, which can include:

  • Administrative Actions: Non-judicial punishment (NJP) under Article 15 of the Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ).
  • Career Consequences: Potential demotion, loss of promotion opportunities, or even discharge.
  • Security Clearance Risks: A DUI can trigger a review of security clearances, which are critical for many military roles.

1. Immediate Reporting Requirements

After a DUI arrest, policyholders must notify GEICO as soon as possible. Failure to report can result in denied claims or policy cancellation. For military personnel, timely reporting is even more critical due to potential chain-of-command notifications.

2. Policy Review and Adjustments

A DUI conviction often leads to:
- Increased Premiums: GEICO may reclassify the driver as high-risk, leading to higher rates.
- SR-22 Requirements: Many states mandate an SR-22 form (proof of financial responsibility) after a DUI. GEICO assists in filing this form but may charge additional fees.
- Possible Policy Non-Renewal: In severe cases, GEICO may choose not to renew the policy.

3. Legal and Financial Support

GEICO does not provide legal representation, but they may offer:
- Referrals to Military Legal Assistance: Many bases have Judge Advocate General (JAG) offices that can help.
- Payment Plans: For service members struggling with increased premiums, GEICO may work out flexible payment options.

Facing a DUI is stressful, but there are steps active-duty personnel can take to mitigate the damage:

1. Seek Legal Counsel Immediately

  • Contact a JAG lawyer or civilian attorney specializing in military DUIs.
  • Understand both civilian and military court proceedings.

2. Communicate with Chain of Command

DUIs remain a persistent issue in the military due to factors like:
