Farmers vs. Allstate Earthquake Policies: Which Wins?

  • Home
  • /
  • Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

When it comes to protecting your home from natural disasters, earthquake insurance is a critical consideration—especially in regions prone to seismic activity. Two of the biggest names in the insurance industry, Farmers Insurance and Allstate, offer earthquake coverage, but how do they stack up against each other? With climate change intensifying natural disasters and urban development expanding into riskier zones, choosing the right policy has never been more important.

Understanding Earthquake Insurance

Before diving into the specifics of Farmers and Allstate, it’s essential to grasp what earthquake insurance covers—and what it doesn’t. Unlike standard homeowners’ insurance, earthquake policies are typically sold as separate endorsements or standalone policies.

What Does Earthquake Insurance Cover?

  • Structural damage to your home
  • Personal property losses (e.g., furniture, electronics)
  • Additional living expenses if your home becomes uninhabitable
  • Masonry and foundation repairs

What’s Usually Excluded?

  • Land movement unrelated to earthquakes (e.g., sinkholes)
  • Pre-existing damage
  • Vehicles (covered under auto insurance instead)

Farmers Insurance Earthquake Coverage

Farmers Insurance, a subsidiary of Zurich Insurance Group, offers earthquake coverage as an endorsement to homeowners’ policies in most states.

Key Features of Farmers’ Earthquake Policy

  • Deductible Structure: Typically 10-20% of the home’s insured value (varies by state).
  • Coverage Limits: Up to the dwelling limit of your homeowners’ policy.
  • Optional Add-Ons: Loss of use, personal property replacement.

Pros of Farmers Earthquake Insurance

✔ Flexible Deductibles: Some policies allow percentage-based or fixed deductibles.
✔ Bundling Discounts: Combining home and auto insurance can lower premiums.
✔ Strong Financial Backing: Zurich’s global presence ensures claim reliability.

Cons of Farmers Earthquake Insurance

✖ High Deductibles: Percentage-based deductibles can be costly for expensive homes.
✖ Limited Availability: Not offered in all high-risk states.

Allstate Earthquake Insurance

Allstate, one of the largest insurers in the U.S., provides earthquake coverage through its "Hostile Earth" endorsement (a unique branding choice).

Key Features of Allstate’s Earthquake Policy

  • Deductible Options: Typically 5-15%, with some policies offering lower deductibles for retrofitted homes.
  • Coverage Extensions: Includes detached structures like garages.
  • Claim Process: Known for a streamlined digital claims system.

Pros of Allstate Earthquake Insurance

✔ Lower Deductibles for Retrofits: Homes with seismic upgrades may qualify for reduced deductibles.
✔ Quick Claims Processing: Allstate’s mobile app speeds up payouts.
✔ Nationwide Availability: More accessible in high-risk states like California.

Cons of Allstate Earthquake Insurance

✖ Stricter Underwriting: Older homes may face higher premiums.
✖ Limited Customization: Fewer add-on options compared to Farmers.

Comparing Farmers vs. Allstate

1. Pricing and Deductibles

  • Farmers: Higher deductibles but more flexibility in bundling.
  • Allstate: Lower deductibles for retrofitted homes, but premiums can be steeper.

2. Coverage Flexibility

  • Farmers: More customizable with optional endorsements.
  • Allstate: Simpler structure but fewer add-ons.

3. Claims Experience

  • Farmers: Strong financial backing but slower claims in disaster surges.
  • Allstate: Faster digital claims but mixed customer reviews on payouts.

4. Availability

  • Farmers: Limited in some high-risk zones.
  • Allstate: More widely available in earthquake-prone areas.

Which One Should You Choose?

The best policy depends on your location, home value, and risk tolerance.

  • For High-Value Homes: Farmers might offer better customization.
  • For Retrofitted Homes: Allstate’s deductible discounts could save money.
  • For Fast Claims: Allstate’s digital tools provide an edge.

The Bigger Picture: Why Earthquake Insurance Matters Now

With rising seismic activity due to fracking, climate-induced geological shifts, and aging infrastructure, the risk of earthquake damage is growing. States like California, Alaska, and even unexpected regions like Oklahoma are seeing increased tremors.

Government Programs vs. Private Insurance

  • California Earthquake Authority (CEA): A state-run option, but private insurers like Farmers and Allstate often provide better service.
  • Federal Disaster Relief: FEMA aid is slow and rarely covers full losses.

Future-Proofing Your Policy

  • Retrofitting: Strengthening your home can lower premiums.
  • Regular Reviews: Adjust coverage as home values and risks change.

In the battle of Farmers vs. Allstate, there’s no clear winner—just the right fit for your needs. Whether you prioritize affordability, flexibility, or fast claims, both insurers offer compelling options in an increasingly shaky world.
